# Six-month Raspberry Pi Pico publishing calendar

Planning window: August 2026 through January 2027. Dates are target Tuesdays and can move to match production capacity. Existing foundation content can be treated as a launch, major refresh, or repromotion assignment.

## Strategy

- Publish prerequisites before the project that uses them.
- Move learners through one kit and five progressively harder builds.
- Pair informational tutorials with project-intent pages and commercially relevant guides.
- Reserve the final month for consolidation, performance-led refreshes, and the next learning-path decision.
- Maintain a sustainable baseline of two major releases per month, with three during launch and consolidation months.

## Monthly operating rhythm

| Week | Activity |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Publish or refresh one supporting tutorial/guide; review prior-month metrics |
| Week 2 | Technical build and media capture for the month's project |
| Week 3 | Publish project; add internal links from supporting content |
| Week 4 | Fix launch findings, test affiliate links, and prepare next month's briefs |

## Measurement schedule

- Enter every live URL in the analytics tracker on publication day.
- Check indexing, metadata, links, and early engagement after 7 days.
- Record full monthly metrics during the first five business days of each month.
- Conduct 30-, 60-, and 90-day reviews for every major page.
- Use the January audit to identify pages with impressions but weak click-through, strong traffic but weak engagement, or commercial clicks without conversions.

## Capacity rule

If production falls behind, protect technical verification and quality gates. Move the target date rather than publishing untested code, inaccurate diagrams, weak disclosure, or incomplete accessibility work.
