## Choose a platform before counting parts

Raspberry Pi Pico is an approachable route into MicroPython and modern microcontroller development. Arduino-compatible UNO boards use C/C++ sketches and benefit from a very large collection of beginner examples and libraries.

Both platforms can teach breadboards, LEDs, buttons, sensors, PWM, and motors. Your first five projects matter more than small differences in component count.

## What a useful kit includes

- A controller board and data-capable USB cable
- Breadboard and a generous set of jumper wires
- LEDs plus several resistor values
- Buttons, a potentiometer, and basic sensors
- At least one display and one actuator such as a servo
- Lessons that identify every pin and explain safe voltage limits

## Avoid buying by component count alone

A huge assortment can become clutter if parts are unlabeled or the tutorial does not match the included board. Prefer organized storage, clear documentation, and components you can immediately use.
