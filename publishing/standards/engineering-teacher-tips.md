# Engineering Teacher Tips

An Engineering Teacher Tip explains the transferable reasoning behind a project choice. It should connect one immediate detail—such as a resistor, shared ground, measurement, polarity check, or power-off inspection—to a habit learners will reuse.

A strong tip:

- explains why the idea matters;
- uses beginner language;
- supports safety, design judgment, or systematic debugging;
- remains concise; and
- does not interrupt the main instructions.

Do not use the box for trivia, promotional copy, or a warning without explanation.
