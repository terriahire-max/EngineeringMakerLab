# Writing Style

Write for a curious beginner who is capable but may not know electronics vocabulary yet. Define a term before relying on it. Prefer concrete actions and observable results over abstractions.

Use short paragraphs, direct sentences, descriptive headings, and practical examples. Explain why a step matters, especially when it concerns safety or debugging. Keep instructions precise enough to follow without assuming hidden knowledge.

All writing must be original. Never copy or lightly rewrite a tutorial, product page, manual, or another publisher's explanation. Summarize only verified facts and link readers to the authoritative source for vendor-specific procedures.
