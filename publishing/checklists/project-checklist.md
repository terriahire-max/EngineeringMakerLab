# Project Checklist

## Content

- [ ] Writing is original and beginner-friendly.
- [ ] The outcome, time, cost, platform, and difficulty are clear.
- [ ] Paragraphs are short and technical terms are defined.
- [ ] Learning goals are measurable.
- [ ] Parts and product claims are verified.
- [ ] Official resources are linked instead of reproduced.
- [ ] Engineering Teacher Tip explains a transferable principle.

## Technical Accuracy

- [ ] Pin assignments match in text, code, circuit, and breadboard diagram.
- [ ] Code has been tested or its untested status is explicit.
- [ ] Voltage, polarity, current limiting, and ground are correct.
- [ ] Troubleshooting covers likely beginner failures safely.

## Visuals and Experience

- [ ] Hero or project preview is complete.
- [ ] Finished-project image is complete.
- [ ] Circuit diagram is present, original, labeled, and readable.
- [ ] Breadboard diagram is present, original, labeled, and readable.
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text and fixed dimensions.
- [ ] Mobile layout and horizontal overflow are reviewed.
- [ ] Keyboard navigation and visible focus states are reviewed.

## Links and Conversion

- [ ] Affiliate buttons use centralized configuration and are verified.
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure is present.
- [ ] Internal links resolve to published routes.
- [ ] Planned projects do not create broken links.
- [ ] Recommendation language is honest and free of hype.
