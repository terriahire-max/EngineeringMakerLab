# Affiliate links checklist

## Editorial independence

- [ ] Product inclusion is supported by project fit, usability, safety, documentation, and value.
- [ ] Ranking criteria were established before affiliate availability was considered.
- [ ] Limitations and viable alternatives are disclosed.
- [ ] Copy contains no false scarcity, guaranteed savings, or unsupported “best” claim.

## Data and implementation

- [ ] Product name, description, image, tags, and vendor URLs are maintained in the central product catalog.
- [ ] Projects and guides reference stable product IDs; retailer URLs are not hardcoded in Markdown.
- [ ] Placeholder or unverified destinations remain hidden from visitors.
- [ ] Each active destination opens the intended product and vendor in the target market.
- [ ] Outbound affiliate links use the approved sponsored/nofollow relationship.
- [ ] Link labels identify the retailer and do not disguise the destination.

## Disclosure and compliance

- [ ] The standard affiliate disclosure appears before or adjacent to the first affiliate action.
- [ ] Disclosure language is clear, readable, and not visually de-emphasized.
- [ ] Prices and availability are omitted unless a dated verification and maintenance plan exist.
- [ ] Vendor program terms, imagery rules, trademarks, and prohibited claims were reviewed.
- [ ] Link testing date, tester, market, and result are recorded.
- [ ] A refresh date is scheduled for buying guides and high-click pages.
