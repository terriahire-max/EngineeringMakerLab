# Photography checklist

## Planning and safety

- [ ] The shot list identifies the hero, completed result, critical steps, orientation details, and proof of operation.
- [ ] The circuit is technically verified before photography begins.
- [ ] Power is disconnected while hands or tools reposition wiring.
- [ ] No unsafe mains wiring, damaged cells, exposed conductors, or misleading connections appear.
- [ ] Personal information, serial numbers, screens, and reflections are reviewed.

## Capture

- [ ] Lighting is even, color is neutral, and glare does not hide labels or component orientation.
- [ ] The work surface is clean and consistent with the brand palette.
- [ ] Focus covers the part of the circuit the caption discusses.
- [ ] Wire colors and board labels remain distinguishable.
- [ ] Hero framing allows 16:9, 4:3, and 1:1 crops where practical.
- [ ] At least one image clearly proves the final result works.

## Processing

- [ ] Crop, exposure, white balance, and rotation are corrected without altering technical truth.
- [ ] Raster images are resized near their maximum display dimensions.
- [ ] Web delivery uses WebP or AVIF when practical; a quality fallback is retained when needed.
- [ ] Camera location and sensitive metadata are removed.
- [ ] Files use lowercase kebab-case names and the approved asset folder.
- [ ] Source, working, and web exports are stored separately.

## Accessibility and rights

- [ ] Alt text explains useful visual information and does not repeat the caption verbatim.
- [ ] A reader can understand critical instructions without relying on the photograph alone.
- [ ] Ownership, license, contributor credit, and release information are recorded.
- [ ] Final dimensions, file size, and rendered sharpness are checked on mobile and desktop.
